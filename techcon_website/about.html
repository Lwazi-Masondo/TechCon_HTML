History

TechCon began in 1996 in New Orleans, Louisiana, as a modest gathering of semiconductor professionals and researchers. Over the years, it has evolved into a premier innovation conference, attracting industry leaders, innovators, and investors from around the globe. The conference has been hosted in various cities across the United States, reflecting its growing prominence and the expanding interests of the tech community.

In 2024, TechCon was held from September 8–10 at the Renaissance Hotel in Austin, Texas. The event featured keynotes, technical sessions, and networking opportunities, with highlights including Deirdre Hanford's keynote on R&D's role in sustainability and a diverse lineup of over 200 SRC Research Scholars.

TechCon's evolution reflects the dynamic nature of the technology industry. From its origins focusing on semiconductor research, it has expanded to encompass a broad range of topics, including artificial intelligence, sustainability, and career agility. The conference continues to serve as a platform for knowledge exchange, collaboration, and the celebration of innovation and excellence in technology.

Mission

TechCon® North America is an annual conference focused on improving high-voltage maintenance practices and asset management for aging electrical grids. Since 1996, it has united utility professionals, engineers, suppliers, and decision-makers to share insights, train, and discuss real-world solutions that enhance grid reliability, safety, and performance.

Guided by principles of technical excellence and practical application, TechCon® promotes innovation, collaboration, and ethical standards across the power industry. Its goal is to empower current and future utility workforces through education, knowledge sharing, and exposure to emerging technologies that address the evolving challenges of electrical infrastructure.
